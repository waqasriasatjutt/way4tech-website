## TL;DR

- **Community** is free, open-source, and genuinely production-ready for most SMBs.
- **Enterprise** adds a polished UI (OWL 2), mobile apps, Studio (no-code builder), and some premium modules (MRP II, accounting automation, marketing automation).
- **For 80% of Pakistan/GCC SMBs, Community + a specialist partner beats Enterprise on cost and flexibility.**

## Where Enterprise genuinely pays off

If you need any of these, pay for Enterprise:

1. **Mobile apps** — Enterprise has polished iOS/Android apps. Community has a PWA that's okay but not great.
2. **Studio** — no-code form/view/report editor. Saves developer time for simple admin tweaks.
3. **MRP II** — Enterprise manufacturing is deeper than Community (subcontracting, MPS, scheduled forecasts).
4. **Advanced accounting** — bank synchronization with 20+ Pakistani/GCC banks, OCR bill capture, follow-up automation.
5. **eLearning** — full LMS with quizzes, certifications, SCORM.

## Where Community wins

- **You have a developer or partner.** Most of what Studio does, a developer can do in 30 minutes of Python.
- **You want to customize deeply.** Community code is fully open. Enterprise has binary-only modules that are painful to extend.
- **Multi-tenant / SaaS model.** Enterprise licensing makes multi-tenant cost-prohibitive. Community is the only sensible choice for running your own SaaS platform.
- **Budget is tight.** Enterprise is $31.10 per user per month (2026 price) + app fees. For 50 users that's $18k/year just for licenses.

## Migration paths

Starting on Community and moving to Enterprise later works fine — Odoo has an official migration path. Going Enterprise → Community is **not supported** officially, but we've done it for clients who realized they were paying for features they never used.
